Why AQI matters here

Runners sustain hard effort 24–48 h at 9,000–14,000 ft, where ventilation rates are already extreme. Smoke risk that's "moderate" at sea level is amplified by altitude and exposure time.

This tool flags caution at sustained AQI ≥101 on course and elevates sharply at ≥151.

Wind & alpine exposure

Thirteen passes above treeline; the high point is Handies Peak at 14,048 ft. Gusts ≥35 mph on exposed ridgelines raise hypothermia and lightning-staging risk; ≥55 mph is a serious exposure hazard.

Fire posture logic

No-go signals: AQI ≥201 on course, a fire perimeter on/adjacent to the route, or extreme wind under a Red Flag Warning.

Heightened: AQI 101–200, a Red Flag Warning, a perimeter within ≈25 mi, or alpine gusts 35–55 mph.
